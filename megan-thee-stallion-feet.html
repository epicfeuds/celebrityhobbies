Biography of Megan Thee Stallion

Megan Thee Stallion, born Megan Jovon Ruth Pete on February 15, 1995, in San Antonio, Texas, is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter. She gained popularity in 2019 with her hit single "Hot Girl Summer" and has since become one of the leading figures in hip-hop. Megan is known for her confidence, lyrical prowess, and distinctive style.

Early Life

Megan was raised in Houston, Texas, where she was influenced by Southern hip-hop culture. Her mother, Holly Thomas, was a rapper, which inspired Megan to pursue a music career from a young age. After her mother's passing in 2019, Megan dedicated her success to her memory.

Career Highlights

Since her debut, Megan Thee Stallion has released multiple chart-topping hits, collaborated with various artists, and won numerous awards, including Grammy Awards. She is celebrated not just for her music but also for her advocacy for body positivity and empowerment.
